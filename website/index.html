GIS Day 2014. Map App Hack Challenge.

Field Correspondent is a collaboration of Simon O'Keefe (Groundtruth) and Herve Senot (Groundtruth, Carbon GIS) for the Brisbane GIS Day 2014 Map App Hack, a nationwide open data competition. Our challenge was to...

develop a great entrepreneurial and creative app that makes use of spatial technology and open data on a smartphone app platform.

In late October 2014, we brainstormed ideas about how we could use the concept of capturing photos from a phone (an exciting new feature in modern mobile browsers) and how it could relate to open data. Field Correspondent was born.

Field Correspondent enables you and the people you invite to capture photos and other information about places you care about - directly from a mobile device. No app installation required!

Want to build a photo library of important historic sites in your town? Select sites from freely available government heritage register and invite community members to capture the photos on their phones.

Want to promote a winery tour in your region? Upload your winery locations and challenge wine lovers to record each winery visit with a photo.

Want to keep a record of maintenance or inspections on your organisation's assets, facilities or vegetation? Open up your asset location data to the web and invite your field workers to log their activities on their phone, including before and after photos.

Want to enable the public to notify your organisation of hazards, property damage and littering they discover directly from their phone? Embed a Field Correspondent map in your website which users can use directly on their mobile device to record the issues that matter to them.

  • no installation required
  • always up-to-date
  • available for all mobile devices (iOS, Android, Windows) and even desktop browsers
  • no restrictions on use of technologies
  • no delay, waiting for app store
